Computational Design & Fabrication Group

We advance the state of the art in computational design, digital fabrication, and human-computer interaction, with a focus on creating tools that empower designers, engineers, and makers to create better, more sustainable products and environments.

Meet Our Team

Our Mission

The CDFG is a multidisciplinary research group at MIT dedicated to exploring the intersection of computer science, design, and manufacturing. Our work spans from fundamental algorithms to real-world applications, with the goal of making digital design and fabrication more accessible, efficient, and creative.

We collaborate with industry partners, academic institutions, and community organizations to translate our research into practical solutions that address pressing societal challenges.

Research Focus Areas

  • Computational Design & Optimization
  • Digital Fabrication & Robotics
  • Sustainable Design & Manufacturing
  • Human-Computer Interaction
  • AI-Assisted Design Tools

Our Team

Our diverse team of researchers, engineers, designers, and students brings a wide range of expertise and perspectives to our work.

Faculty & Researchers

Sarah Johnson

Professor & Group Lead

Computational Design | MIT EECS

About

Dr. Johnson is a Professor of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at MIT. Her research focuses on developing computational tools for design and fabrication, with applications in architecture, product design, and sustainable manufacturing. She received her PhD from Stanford University and has published over 100 peer-reviewed papers in top journals and conferences.

Michael Chen

Senior Research Scientist

Robotics & Fabrication | MIT CSAIL

About

Dr. Chen is a Senior Research Scientist at the MIT Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL). His work focuses on the development of robotic fabrication systems and human-robot collaboration in design and manufacturing. He has extensive experience in developing custom hardware and software solutions for digital fabrication processes.
